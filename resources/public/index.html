Solar System

Mercury

Mercury is the smallest planet in our solar system, located closest to the Sun. It has a barren, rocky surface.

Venus

Venus is often referred to as Earth's "sister planet" due to its similar size and composition. However, it has a thick, toxic atmosphere.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only known planet to support life. It has a diverse range of ecosystems.

Mars

Mars is often called the "Red Planet" due to its reddish appearance. It has a thin atmosphere and is a focus of astrobiology.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in the solar system and consists mainly of gas. It has a turbulent atmosphere and a strong magnetic field.

Saturn

Saturn is known for its spectacular ring system, which consists of ice and rock particles. It's a gas giant like Jupiter.

Uranus

Uranus is unique among the planets because it rotates on its side. It's an ice giant with a pale blue-green appearance.

Neptune

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun and is another ice giant. It has a dynamic atmosphere and is known for its Great Dark Spot.
